Co-designed a dynamic and interactive game of lights and colors – Disco Tubes: to win the game, users move and flip the color-lighting cubes among different pillars to match the color pattern on display panel before pillars rise to the top. This project was based on ME218A Smart Product Design class at Stanford This video shows the final resolution of my team's idea about disco tubes, which was intended to be much fancier (e.g. nine pillars rather than five, more agile and smart movement, more swift color-rendering...). Making something under time pressure is different than having a feasible idea. The design skills gained in the project were rewarding, as well as teamwork and project management.

Investigated into the product "electric stove" in terms of "good" and "bad" in ME314 (Good Products, Bad Products) at Stanford. In this project, In order to weave a story and build in our insights about the user experience of a particular product, I put together this discovery video to communicate our initial findings about electric stove - the product we chose to investigate into

Co-designed the CTO communication satellite core structure which satisfied unmet needs of testing engineers and dramatically improved satellite production process, teamed up between Stanford and Universidad Nacional Autonoma de Mexico; Based on Project‐based Engineering Design class and sponsored by Lockheed Martin.
This is a storytelling video of the project outcome. The insights we got through many iterations of low-resolution prototyping and constant empathy with testing engineers made an impact to our liaison at Lockheed Martin.

Slinky Dog is a paper robot that my team and I did in a one-week long exercise based on Arduino in a innovation design course at Stanford. The purpose of the exercise was simply to show how easy it was to pick up the intimidating robotic/mechatronic skills.
