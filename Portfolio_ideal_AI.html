Survey Studies on Ideal Human-AI Interaction

TAG: Artificial Intelligence, Human-centered AI, Survey Study, Cultural Psychology, Cross-cultural Study


CHI'24: PAPER LINK
Xiao Ge*, Chunchen Xu*, Daigo Misaki, Hazel Rose Markus, and Jeanne L Tsai. 2024. How Culture Shapes What People Want From AI. In Proceedings of the CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI ’24) , May 11–16, 2024, Honolulu, HI, USA. ACM, New York, NY, USA, 15 pages. https://doi.org/10.1145/3613904.3642660
WHAT:
Postdoc research
WHY:
In the US, AI products tend to reflect the cultural values pervasive in European American contexts. There is an urgent need to incorporate the perceptions, imaginings, concerns, and creativity of diverse groups and communities into AI developments.
HOW:
Survey studies using cultural models of the self and the environment (Markus and Kitayama's work)
IMPACT:
We hope to inspire more fundamental research in the field of HCI to understand the processes of sociocultural construction of technology with the goal to ultimately represent and serve a broader range of the population in society.
